About Aevia Lab

What is Aevia Lab?

Aevia Lab is a research-focused entity working at the intersection of computational biology and precision nutrition. We translate peer-reviewed nutrigenomic and bio-gerontology science into accessible, non-invasive cellular optimization tools.

Is Aevia Lab a clinical or medical organization?

No. Aevia Lab is not a clinic, hospital, healthcare provider, or pharmaceutical company. We publish educational research and develop wellness-optimization software. We do not diagnose, treat, cure or prevent disease. See our Regulatory & Scientific Disclaimer.

Who is Aevia Lab for?

Our content is intended for adults interested in understanding the molecular basis of healthspan — how nutrition, fasting timing, and bioactive compounds influence cellular processes such as autophagy, oxidative balance and mitochondrial output.

The Science

What is nutrigenomics?

Nutrigenomics is the bidirectional study of how the molecules in food influence gene expression and how individual genetic variation modifies the body's response to nutrients. Where classical nutrition treats food as fuel, nutrigenomics treats food as biological signalling.

What is partial epigenetic reprogramming?

Partial epigenetic reprogramming refers to interventions that reset age-associated chemical marks on DNA — methylation patterns, histone modifications — without erasing cellular identity. Recent FDA clearances have moved this field from theoretical to clinical for the first time.

Aevia Lab does not perform reprogramming therapy. We focus on dietary and metabolic levers that influence the same gene-expression pathways non-invasively.

What is autophagy?

Autophagy is the cell's intrinsic recycling program. It degrades damaged proteins, dysfunctional organelles and senescent components, recycling them into amino acids the cell can reuse. It is upregulated by metabolic stress including fasting, exercise and certain phytochemicals. See our Science page.

What is Urolithin A?

Urolithin A is a postbiotic metabolite produced when gut bacteria break down ellagitannins — compounds found in pomegranate, walnuts and certain berries. It has been shown to selectively trigger mitophagy and improve mitochondrial function in clinical trials with older adults.

Should I start fasting based on what I read here?

Always consult a qualified healthcare provider before starting a fasting protocol, particularly if you take medication, are pregnant or breastfeeding, have a history of disordered eating, or manage a metabolic condition. Aevia Lab content is educational, not prescriptive.
